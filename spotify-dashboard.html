Introduction

Music has always been central to my life: vinyl, CDs, live shows. But I wanted to go deeper: has my listening stayed curious and exploratory over time, or have I quietly retreated into the same artists on repeat?

This project turns ten years of personal Spotify streaming history into an interactive Tableau dashboard. Raw JSON data was ingested into MongoDB, cleaned and enriched into an analytics-ready dataset using Python, then visualized in Tableau — treating streaming history as a longitudinal record of musical identity.

Data Pipeline

The pipeline is structured in two layers. Raw JSON files are first loaded as-is into a MongoDB collection — a permanent source of truth that allows transformations to be reproduced without re-importing data. A second collection is then generated using MongoDB aggregation pipelines, producing a cleaned, feature-engineered dataset ready for Tableau.

Cleaning & Transformation

Podcast and audiobook entries are removed to keep the dataset music-only. Fields like IP address and username are dropped for privacy. Timestamps are standardized, field names cleaned, and extremely short playback events filtered out.

Aggregated Collections

Three analytics collections are built on top of the cleaned data. Artist-year metrics track stream count, listening time, unique tracks, skip and shuffle rates, and a discovery indicator flagging an artist's first appearance. Song-year metrics capture the same at the track level, enabling analysis of song longevity and repetition. Monthly summary metrics aggregate activity by month, surfacing trends in exploration and listening identity over time.

Artist Engagement Score

A composite score (0–1) combining listening intensity, completion rate, intentional listening (low shuffle), and track diversity — quantifying how deeply I engaged with an artist in a given year, not just how often I played them.

Listening Behavior Clustering

K-means clustering segments streaming sessions into behavioral groups using shuffle, skip, and playback duration as features. A 5-cluster solution was selected based on elbow method and silhouette scores, balancing cluster quality with interpretability.
